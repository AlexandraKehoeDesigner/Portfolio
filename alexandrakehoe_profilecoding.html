Coding



Coding this Portfolio

Introduction

I designed this portfolio from scratch, using some bootstrap and the help of Google. I wanted to personalize my portfolio while improving on my coding skills. Developing this whole site was a challenge but it was an excellent experience to gain and improve my skills.




The Challenge

Briefly in college I studied HTML/ CSS. At my work I dabbled and fixed websites here an there. But it wasn't until this site that I started from nothing. Not even an idea in mind.



The Process

I would like to say this portfolio came to mind instantly and I coded with ease. But just like anything that is valuable it took hard work, frustration, and joy.
I started with the UX/ UI page but the first time I wrote it it was just a simple one page site that touched on some of the projects I did. My content simple and same with my photos.
Instantly I started usability testing. I asked my teacher fellow friends, asking for insights and ideas.
Pulling from insights from the testing I realized I needed to create a multiple page site: About Me, Coding, and an Art page.
I wanted to show off my vast skill set while also having easy to use navigation.

Mini challenges

Creating this site had many mini challenges that made me learn to persevere, adapt,and keep moving forward.

  • Designing the header: I created three different headers for the homepage and didn't multiple A/B testing to finally settle on the one I have now
  • Javascript: Starting out this class I have only briefly touched on javascript. However I wanted to add a carousel to the homepage, many hours I finally got my carousel.
  • Organization: I have always been a decent organized person. But working on my many coding projects made me realized how important it is to keep all files in order.
Through the process of designing this portfolio I learned more about coding in a month than I ever thought I could. It also sparked a passion within me to keep learning and improving.
